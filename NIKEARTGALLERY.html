About Nigerian Nike Art Gallery

The Nigerian Nike Art Gallery, located in Lagos, Nigeria, is a renowned art gallery founded by Nike Davies-Okundaye. It showcases a rich collection of Nigerian art, including traditional and contemporary works.

Facts about the Gallery

  • The gallery was established in 1983 by Nike Davies-Okundaye, a prominent Nigerian artist and textile designer.
  • It is one of the largest art galleries in Nigeria, spanning multiple floors and exhibiting a diverse range of artworks.
  • The gallery primarily focuses on promoting Nigerian art and preserving traditional Nigerian artistic practices.
  • Nike Davies-Okundaye is known for her expertise in traditional Nigerian textile arts, particularly adire (indigo-dyed cloth) and batik.
  • The gallery offers workshops and training programs to support local artists and artisans, contributing to the preservation of Nigerian art and culture.
  • Visitors to the gallery can experience live demonstrations of traditional Nigerian art techniques, such as weaving, dyeing, and beadwork.
